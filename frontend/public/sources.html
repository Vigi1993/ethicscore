Tier 3 includes blogs, specialist publications, and secondary sources. Used only as supporting evidence alongside higher-tier sources — never as the sole basis for a score. Evaluated case-by-case, no fixed whitelist.
How we score brands

Each brand is evaluated on 20 criteria across 4 equal categories (25 pts each). Every criterion is rated 1–5 based on evidence from verified sources. Score 3 means no evidence found — neutral, never penalized.

Final score & rating bands

The final score is the average of the 4 category scores (0–100 each). It reflects only criteria for which evidence has been found and verified.

90–100
Deeply Ethical
Consistent virtuous behavior across all dimensions, documented by multiple verified sources.
75–89
Fairly Ethical
Strong ethical record with minor or isolated criticisms.
55–74
Partially Ethical
Mixed evidence: positive practices coexist with documented problems.
35–54
Scarcely Ethical
Multiple documented issues across categories, limited evidence of improvement.
0–34
Ethically Compromised
Serious, systematic violations documented across multiple dimensions.
Every score change requires human review. New sources — whether found automatically or proposed by the community — are never applied directly. They go through a manual approval step before affecting any brand score. If you know a reliable source we're missing, write to us.
